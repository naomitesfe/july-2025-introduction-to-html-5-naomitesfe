Overview

Women have played a critical role in the advancement of technology, from the early days of computing to today's cutting-edge innovations. Despite challenges, many continue to lead, inspire, and transform the tech industry.

Trailblazing Women in Tech

Ada Lovelace

Recognized as the first computer programmer, Ada Lovelace worked on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.

Grace Hopper

A computer scientist and U.S. Navy rear admiral, Grace Hopper developed the first compiler for a computer programming language and helped pioneer COBOL.

Modern Innovators

From CEOs to developers, women today are leading startups, conducting groundbreaking research, and advocating for inclusive tech culture around the globe.
